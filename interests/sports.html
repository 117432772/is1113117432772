Why Having Sport as a Hobby is Important

Exercise improves learning, memory, and concentration, which can give active students an advantage when it comes to exam time.

Those who play sports learn teamwork and goal-setting skills. Being a team player can make it easier to work with others and solve problems, whether on the field or in the workplace.

Sports have hidden health benefits. Those who play sports are less likely to smoke and have a reduced chance of getting physical illnesses osteoporosis later in life.

Playing sports builds self-confidence. Those involved in athletics feel better about themselves, both physically and socially. It helps to build confidence when you see your skills improving and your goals becoming reality. Other esteem-boosting benefits of sports participation include getting in shape, maintaining a healthy weight, and making new friends.
